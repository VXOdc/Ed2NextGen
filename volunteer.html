How it works

Clear steps for students, chapters, and schools.

Volunteers join a project, complete approved educational work, record their time, and receive verification after review. Chapter leaders can organize teams and help students build consistent service habits.

  1. Apply Tell us your grade, interests, and availability.
  2. Create Write, design, illustrate, research, or review learning materials.
  3. Log Submit dates, tasks, hours, and a short description of your work.
  4. Verify Approved work can be used for school service records.

Submission Guidelines

What we look for in every resource

1

Accepted resource types

Children's books, science explainers, history guides, language learning materials, activity books, worksheets, and study guides for K–12 learners.

2

Formatting requirements

Submit work as PDF or Google Doc. Include a title page with your name and school. Use clear headings, readable fonts, and age-appropriate language.

3

Quality standards

Content must be accurate, well-organized, and educational. All facts should be verified. Work is reviewed before publication.

4

Original work only

All submissions must be your own original work. Cite any sources, images, or references you use.

5

AI policy

AI may be used for brainstorming and generating images, but the final work must be created, reviewed, and owned by the student. AI-generated text submitted as-is will not be accepted.
